## Journal

Three days into peak week and the body is giving a clear progress report: fatigued, but moving. The competition squat at 182.5 for a triple came in at RPE 8 — not the crisp, dominant set the program targets, but three reps with depth locked in and the bar on my back where it belongs. Slow out of the hole on every rep, the kind of slow that tells you the nervous system is running a deficit, but it moved and it counted. The tempo sets at 147.5 and 150 were their usual punishment — five slow reps under a tempo that makes moderate loads feel barbaric — but form held across both sets. Bench was the more encouraging story: climbed 147.5, 150, 152.5, 155 across four competition sets of three, and 155 for a triple at RPE 8.5 is the kind of number that means something heading into July. The last rep at 155 was a genuine fight but it went. Accessories were maintenance — chest flies and lateral raises kept clean and controlled, nothing heroic, just enough to finish the session with something in the tank. Two down on the heavy side this week, one more to go.
