## Journal

Monday deadlift day, and the body still hadn't cleared the debt from Sunday's disastrous squat session. Coming in already fatigued on the DL-specific fatigue scale and the bar confirmed it immediately: three cluster sets at 192.5 kg, all three feeling labored in a way that weight shouldn't feel labored this close to competition. The first two sets I just ground through at RPE 8 and 8.5 — no bar speed, no pop off the floor, just grinding. Third set I took more patience with the cluster rest and it actually moved slightly better, but then my chest cramped so violently partway through the fourth rep that I racked it at three and genuinely thought something had torn. Bench tempo afterwards was a slog — 127.5 for the first two sets with bad pauses, got 130 on the last set and the first three reps were the best of the session, then lost the pause on the fourth. Accessories were just damage control: got through the hip thrusts and lat pulldowns, kept the reps clean, and called it. Two consecutive rough days in peak week is not ideal data. The plan now is to eat well tonight, sleep as long as the body wants, and let the taper work.

*Session reflection:* Deadlifts at 192.5 feeling that heavy this late in the block is a red flag worth acknowledging — the chest cramp that killed the third cluster set was the body waving a white flag. Trust the taper, trust the protein, show up rested for Day 3. 🪨

After a session like this, protein is non-negotiable — 200 g minimum tonight, because cramped muscles and a system that's been running hard for four weeks need every gram they can get to come back ready.
