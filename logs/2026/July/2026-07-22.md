## Journal

Day 4 and last training session of peak week — deadlift day, the way peak week always ends. Walked in already running on fumes after three consecutive heavy sessions, but the bar had a different feel today: competition pulls with full rest, no cluster format, just stand, brace, pull. Worked up from 185 to 192.5 for sets of four, and that third set at 192.5 was the hardest thing I've done this week — every gram of it. The hips shot up a little on the first set, rep 3 had a hitch on the second, and by the time the fourth rep locked out at 192.5 I was done. But it locked out. After Monday's cluster sets at 192.5 that felt like fighting gravity at twice its normal strength, today's rep quality was better — more patient off the floor, more leg drive in the first half of the pull. Accessories were cleanup work: chest supported rows, hamstring curls, alternating DB curls, everything kept deliberate and in the right RPE range. The lat rows at 70 kg/side on the last set were genuinely challenging, which is either a sign of accumulated fatigue or the curls that came after it. The competition is approaching fast — the prep is in the bank, and now the body just needs days off.

Protein is the last non-negotiable of peak week: 200 g minimum tonight, because the deadlift sets and accessories that just ran through this system don't repair on good intentions alone.

*Session reflection:* 192.5 kg for a competition-style quad at RPE 8 on Day 4 of peak week is the kind of set that will look easy in hindsight once the comp weight hits the bar. The prep is done — time to rest and trust it. 🪨
