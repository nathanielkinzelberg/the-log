## Journal

Day 4 of Block 6, Week 1 — deadlift and accessories, and the bar moved like it had something to prove. Three ascending competition deadlift sets at 177.5, 182, and 185 kg, all for 4 reps, and none of them felt like they cost anything — every set got labeled warmup or close to it, which is exactly how you want the opener week of a new block to feel. The feedback from the sheet said it best: deadlifts moving great. Lat rows on the chest-supported machine were solid across three sets at 180–185 kg with RPE climbing from 7 to 8.5, which is the right progression for a strength accessory. The hamstrings got punished on leg curls — 99 kg and then two sets at 92.3 with slow eccentrics had RPE at 9 both rounds, and the bicep curls at the end were fully maximal, RPE 10 on the first set with 20 kg and then 18 kg for the remaining two.

Protein hit right after the session — meat and eggs to feed those hammies and keep the adaptation train rolling.

💪 Block 6, Day 4 done. Bar speed on the deadlift is a good sign this early in the block.

*Session reflection:* When the heaviest planned set feels like a warmup, that's not complacency — that's the program working. The body adapted, and now you go build on it.
