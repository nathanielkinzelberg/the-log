## Journal

Block 6, Week 4, Day 1 — two full rest days and the body came back with nothing, which is the kind of feedback that doesn't get easier to read when the numbers are in front of you. The squats were the clearest read: 170, 162.5, 160 kg for 5 reps each, descending because the first set at 170 already came in at RPE 8 against a target of 6.5, and every reduction after that still landed at 8 — the body wouldn't let the bar move the way it should move when the program is working, regardless of what the weight on the bar said. Bench hit the planned range (145–147.5–150 kg) but the top set at 150 came in at RPE 9.5, which is a number that belongs to a PR attempt, not a Week 4 Day 1 working set. Accessories backed off entirely: 32 kg DBs on the chest press instead of the standard load, triceps stuck at the same cable weight with form falling apart by the second set. The session note says it plainly — one of the weakest days in months, went lighter on accessories because form was breaking down. Two rest days cleared the visible fatigue but not the debt underneath it.
